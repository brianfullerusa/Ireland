Galway & the West

The wild Atlantic edge

Galway was granted its city charter in 1484, and the medieval street plan hasn't changed much since. Known as the "City of the Tribes" after the fourteen merchant families who ruled it for centuries, it's now Ireland's cultural heart — a bilingual city where you'll hear Irish spoken in the streets and see it on every sign. Shop Street and Quay Street form a pedestrian spine of buskers, oyster bars, and pubs where traditional sessions break out most nights without a schedule. Beyond the city, the landscape shifts fast: the lunar limestone of the Burren, the wild Connemara coast, and the Aran Islands — where stone walls outnumber people and Irish is still the first language.
