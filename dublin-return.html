Return to Dublin

One last night in the city

The drive back east crosses the Golden Vale — Ireland's richest farmland, the green patchwork you picture when you hear the word "Ireland." With a 3:25 PM Sunday flight and US Preclearance at Dublin Airport, there's plenty of time for one last evening in the city center — a proper farewell dinner, a morning walk along the Grand Canal, and a quality brunch before heading to the airport. End the trip the way it deserves to end.

September 5 — Return & Farewell

9:30 AM

Depart Killarney

Two cars caravanning north on the N22 toward Limerick, then M7/M8 to Dublin. Fuel up before leaving Killarney.

~11:30 AM

Comfort stop — Barack Obama Plaza, Moneygall

M7 motorway services at the halfway mark. Good coffee, clean facilities, and an amusing photo op at the Obama statue. 15–20 minutes.

1:30 PM

Arrive Dublin & check in

Check in at the hotel. Drop bags. Park both cars in the hotel's designated car park (Q-Park on Pearse Street if Trinity City, valet at The Shelbourne/Merrion). Overnight parking ~15–25 EUR per car.

2:00 PM

Decompress

Rest at the hotel, freshen up after the drive. Some may nap; others can stroll St. Stephen's Green or Grafton Street.

3:30 PM

Optional last wander

Grafton Street, Powerscourt Townhouse Centre, or just sit in the hotel bar with a cup of tea and let the trip sink in.

7:00 PM

Farewell dinner — Chapter One by Mickael Viljanen

Parnell Square, in the basement of the Dublin Writers Museum. Michelin star. Vaulted brick ceilings, warm lighting, serious cooking without pretension. The closest Dublin equivalent to Bouchon — impeccable ingredients, French technique with Irish produce. Tasting menu ~85 EUR or a la carte ~80–120 EUR per person + wine. Book well in advance. 15-minute taxi from St. Stephen's Green.

Swap options for dinner

The Winding Stair (Ormond Quay) — modern Irish overlooking the Ha'penny Bridge, ~35–45 EUR mains. Dax (Pembroke Street) — French-Irish fine dining in a Georgian basement, natural fit for those who love Bouchon. Locks Brasserie (Portobello) — canal-side French-Irish brasserie, ~30–40 EUR mains.

9:30 PM

Farewell pint — Kehoe's or The Long Hall

Kehoe's (South Anne Street) — genuine Dublin institution, Victorian interior, no music, just great pints and conversation. The Long Hall (South Great George's Street) — one of Dublin's most beautiful pub interiors, ornate Victorian, old-world atmosphere. Both are 10 minutes from St. Stephen's Green. Those who are tired head straight back — no pressure.

Sunday

September 6 — Departure

7:30 AM

Wake up

Last morning in Ireland.

8:00 AM

Morning walk — The Grand Canal Loop

Walk south to the Grand Canal at Baggot Street Bridge. Follow the towpath east to Grand Canal Dock (~1.5 miles). Loop back through Merrion Square (Oscar Wilde statue, Georgian doors at their finest). Return via St. Stephen's Green for a final lap. Total: ~3–3.5 miles, 75 minutes at a comfortable pace. Quiet, flat, gorgeous September morning light along the water.

9:30 AM

Brunch at Cliff Townhouse

St. Stephen's Green North. The "Full Cliff" breakfast: Glenilen Farm eggs, Gubbeen chorizo, Jack McCarthy sausages, Clonakilty black pudding, proper brown bread, excellent coffee. Every ingredient has a story — the kind of quality that makes the last meal of the trip count. Call ahead for a party of 7. Allow 60–75 minutes.

Swap options for brunch

Hatch & Sons (St. Stephen's Green) — organic Irish breakfast, farmhouse sausages, proper brown bread. Opens 9:30 AM. The Woollen Mills (Ormond Quay) — house-baked bread, excellent eggs, Liffey views, opens 10:00 AM. Brother Hubbard (Capel Street) — Middle Eastern-Irish fusion, shakshuka, Turkish eggs. Local favorite, opens 9:30 AM.

10:45 AM

Final packing & checkout

Settle the bill, retrieve cars from the car park.

11:15 AM

Depart for Dublin Airport

Everyone rides to the airport. Drop passengers at Terminal 2 departures, then both drivers proceed to rental car return (well-signposted, ~3 minutes from the terminal). Cars returned in 15–20 minutes. Drivers walk or shuttle back to Terminal 2.

11:45 AM

Arrive Dublin Airport

Drop 5 passengers with luggage at Terminal 2. Drivers return both rental cars and rejoin the group at Aer Lingus check-in by ~12:15.

12:15 PM

Check in & US Preclearance

Dublin is one of the few airports with US Customs and Border Protection on-site. You clear US immigration here — meaning you arrive home as a domestic flight with no immigration lines on the other end. Budget 60–90 minutes for check-in, bag drop, and Preclearance.

1:30 PM

Airside

Browse duty free, grab a coffee, relax at the gate. The hard part is done.

3:25 PM

Depart on EI 59

Fly home. Arrive in the US having already cleared customs. Trip complete.
