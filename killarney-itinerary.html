The recommended plan

Killarney & Kerry — Three Day Itinerary

Based on the travel agent panel's recommendations across all six reviewed activities. Three nights based in Killarney town, September 2–4, 2026.

Wednesday

September 2 — Arrival from Galway

9:00 AM

Depart Galway

Leave after a relaxed hotel breakfast. The drive to Killarney via the M18 and N21 through Limerick takes approximately 3 to 3.5 hours. Consider a brief stop in Adare (15 minutes off the N21) to stretch legs and see its famous thatched cottages.

12:00 PM

Lunch stop in Adare or Limerick

If stopping in Adare, the Wild Geese Restaurant or the 1826 Adare offer excellent food in a charming village. Otherwise, grab lunch after arriving in Killarney.

1:30 PM

Arrive in Killarney & check in

Check in to the hotel. Killarney has excellent options in every range — The Killarney Park Hotel, The Europe Hotel, or The Ross Hotel are all well-positioned for the town center. Unpack and settle in.

2:30 PM

Hotel rest

Decompress after the drive. 60–90 minutes. Everyone benefits from arriving fresh for the first Killarney evening.

4:00 PM

Ross Castle & Lake Tour

A 10-minute drive or jaunting car ride from town. The 15th-century tower house sits on the shore of Lough Leane with spectacular lake and mountain views. Take the guided tour (30 minutes) and then walk the lakeshore path — flat, paved, and as long or short as you like. Back by 6:00 PM.

6:30 PM

Dinner in Killarney town

Bricin Restaurant on High Street for traditional Kerry dishes (boxty, Kerry lamb), or The Laurels on Main Street for reliable pub food with live music from 9:00 PM. Book ahead for groups of 7.

9:00 PM

First night in the Killarney pubs

Courtney's Bar on Plunkett Street is a tiny, authentic gem — no TVs, no gimmicks, just conversation and traditional music most nights. The Danny Mann on New Street is livelier with nightly trad sessions. Anyone who prefers a quieter wind-down can return to the hotel after dinner.

Thursday

September 3 — Killarney National Park Day

9:00 AM

Muckross House & Gardens

A 10-minute drive from town. Arrive early to beat the tour buses. The Victorian mansion tour takes 45 minutes; the gardens and lakeside grounds are free to explore. The Traditional Farms nearby recreate 1930s Kerry rural life — charming and unhurried. Allow 2 hours total.

11:30 AM

Torc Waterfall & Ladies' View

From Muckross, it is a 5-minute drive to Torc Waterfall. The walk from the car park to the falls is about 200 meters on a well-maintained path. After the waterfall, continue on the N71 for 15 minutes to Ladies' View — one of the most photographed panoramas in Ireland, overlooking the three Lakes of Killarney. Moll's Gap is another 5 minutes beyond. Allow 90 minutes for this stretch.

1:00 PM

Lunch

Avoca Cafe at Moll's Gap has excellent food with panoramic mountain views — soups, salads, and baked goods. Alternatively, return to Killarney town for lunch at Jam on High Street or Murphy's Ice Cream on Main Street (yes, it is a meal).

2:30 PM

Hotel rest

Essential midday break. 90 minutes. Recharge before the afternoon outing.

4:00 PM

Jaunting car ride through the National Park

The iconic Killarney experience. Horse-drawn jaunting cars depart from the Muckross House car park or from the town center. A 1-hour ride through the park takes you along the lake, through ancient oak woodlands, and past the ruins of Muckross Abbey. The jarveys (drivers) are legendary storytellers. Fits up to 4 per car — book 2 cars for the group.

6:00 PM

Early dinner at Treyvaud's

Run by two brothers who trained in some of Europe's top kitchens. Modern Irish food, reasonable prices, excellent wine list. On High Street, walkable from every hotel. Book for 6:00 PM — it fills fast.

8:30 PM

O'Connor's Traditional Pub

On High Street. One of Killarney's best-loved locals' pubs with live music nightly. A proper session pub — not a tourist show. The group can drop in for a pint or two and let the evening unfold naturally.

Friday

September 4 — Dingle Peninsula Day Trip

8:30 AM

Depart for Dingle Peninsula

Leave Killarney heading west on the N70/R561. The drive to Dingle town takes about 1 hour 20 minutes via the Conor Pass (weather permitting) — one of Ireland's highest mountain passes with staggering views. If conditions are poor, take the lower coast road via Castlemaine and Inch Beach.

10:00 AM

Arrive in Dingle town

A small, colorful fishing town on the tip of the peninsula. Wander the main street, duck into Dick Mack's pub (a legendary pub-and-hardware-shop hybrid), and have a coffee at Bean in Dingle. The harbor has a bronze statue of Fungie the dolphin — the beloved bottlenose who lived in Dingle Bay for 37 years until 2020.

11:00 AM

Slea Head Drive

The 30-mile loop from Dingle town around the tip of the peninsula is one of Europe's most spectacular coastal drives. Highlights include the Beehive Huts (Clochan), Dunmore Head (the westernmost point of mainland Ireland), the Blasket Islands viewpoint, Gallarus Oratory (a perfectly preserved early Christian stone church from the 7th century), and Coumeenoole Beach. Allow 2.5–3 hours with stops.

1:30 PM

Lunch back in Dingle

Out of the Blue on the waterfront is widely considered the best seafood restaurant in Kerry — fish landed that morning, no meat on the menu. For something quicker, Chowder on Strand Street does superb fish and chips and seafood chowder. Murphy's Ice Cream (the Dingle original) is a required stop.

2:30 PM

After-lunch rest in Dingle

Dingle is an easy town to rest in. Sit on the harbor wall, browse the craft shops on Green Street, or simply enjoy a second coffee. No rush — the group sets its own pace before the drive back.

3:30 PM

Return drive to Killarney

Take the lower coast road back via Inch Beach (a long sandy strand worth a 10-minute stop) and Castlemaine. About 1 hour 15 minutes. Arrive by 5:00 PM.

5:00 PM

Hotel rest

Final evening wind-down before the last Killarney night.

7:00 PM

Final dinner — The Cellar at the International Hotel

A Killarney institution on Kenmare Place. Classic bistro menu with Kerry beef and local seafood. Great wine cellar. Alternatively, Cronin's Restaurant on High Street for a more relaxed, family-run experience.

9:30 PM

Final pub night

Courtney's, The Danny Mann, or wherever the music is best. Last night in Kerry — let it breathe.

Saturday

September 5 — Departure to Dublin

8:00 AM

Leisurely breakfast, pack up

Check out of the hotel. No rush — the drive to Dublin is about 3.5 hours via the M7/M8.

9:30 AM

Gap of Dunloe morning walk Optional add-on

If the group wants a taste of the Gap before departing, a short morning walk into the first section of the Gap of Dunloe (Kate Kearney's Cottage to the first bridge, about 30 minutes each way) is possible before heading to Dublin. Otherwise, depart directly for Dublin.

10:00 AM

Depart Killarney for Dublin

~3.5 hours via the M7/M8. Stop in Cashel to see the Rock of Cashel from the road (or do a 30-minute visit if time allows). Arrive at Dublin city center hotel by early afternoon.
