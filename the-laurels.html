Alternative evening —

The Laurels

Killarney activity review • Alternative to Bricin for Wednesday evening

Snapshot

The Laurels is a dependable pub and restaurant on Main Street in Killarney, popular with both locals and visitors for its combination of solid pub food and nightly live music. It occupies a prime location in the center of town and offers something that neither Bricin nor Courtney's can match on their own: dinner and entertainment in a single venue. The food is honest pub fare — steaks, seafood, burgers, and traditional Irish dishes — served in generous portions. Live music kicks off around 9:00 PM most nights, with a mix of traditional and popular Irish songs. For a group that wants a more casual evening without moving between a restaurant and a pub, The Laurels is a practical and enjoyable one-stop option.

Why it made the list

  • The only venue on the Wednesday list that offers both dinner and live music in one place — no need to move between venues.
  • Dependable pub food that satisfies a group with varied tastes — something for everyone on the menu.
  • The live music from 9:00 PM means the evening transitions naturally from dinner to entertainment.
  • Good for groups — enough space to seat everyone together, and a relaxed atmosphere.
  • Prime Main Street location, walkable from any accommodation in town.

Common review themes

What reviewers love

  • The combination of food and music is consistently praised — "dinner and a show without the fuss."
  • Generous portions and reliable quality — reviewers describe it as "honest food, well done."
  • The musicians are entertaining and engaging, often interacting with the audience.
  • Staff handle large groups well and keep the evening moving smoothly.

Common complaints

  • The food is good but not exceptional — it's pub food, not fine dining. Don't expect Bricin-level cooking.
  • The music can lean toward crowd-pleasing popular songs rather than pure traditional — depends on the night.
  • Gets busy and loud once the music starts — dine early if you want conversation over dinner.
  • Some reviewers find it more tourist-oriented than smaller, quieter pubs.
