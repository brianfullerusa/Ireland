The recommended plan

Dublin — Three Day Itinerary

Based on the travel agent panel’s recommendations across all reviewed activities.

Saturday

August 29 — Arrival

11:45 AM

Land at Dublin Airport

EI 58 from Denver arrives 11:45 AM. Allow 45–60 min for immigration and baggage.

1:30 PM

Check in at Trinity City Hotel

Pre-booked minivan transfer. Hotel will have rooms ready for an early check-in.

2:30 PM

Late lunch near the hotel

Fallon & Byrne deli, Avoca Café, or the Bank on College Green.

3:30 PM

Wander Grafton Street & surrounds

Unstructured explore time. Stroll Grafton Street, duck into Powerscourt Townhouse Centre, browse side streets. Buskers, flower sellers, cafés — let Dublin reveal itself. This is how the best travel memories happen.

4:30 PM

Chester Beatty Library

Free, quiet, fully accessible. Perfect jet-lag afternoon. 8-min walk from hotel, 90 minutes inside. Skip the castle tour this trip.

7:00 PM

Dinner at The Winding Stair

Ormond Quay, overlooking the Ha’penny Bridge. Modern Irish cooking with daily-changing menu — pan-fried hake, slow-braised pork, farmhouse cheeses. Quality ingredients from named Irish producers. Book in advance for 7. ~35–45 EUR per main.

9:00 PM

Dusk walk along the quays + nightcap at The Stag’s Head

Walk back along the Liffey as the light fades — one of Dublin’s most atmospheric strolls. Cut through Dame Court to The Stag’s Head, Dublin’s most beautiful Victorian pub (stained glass, carved mahogany, mosaic floor). One pint, then back to the hotel — 5 min walk. Anyone who’s fading can head straight back.

Swap option: The Brazen Head

Ireland’s oldest pub (1198). If the group prefers a classic pub dinner for night one, The Brazen Head is the atmospheric alternative — book 6:30 PM, order safe classics, leave by 8:30–9:00 PM.

Sunday

August 30 — Full Day

7:30 AM

Early morning walk — Grand Canal

For early risers (jet lag helps): walk from the hotel south to the Grand Canal towpath at Baggot Street Bridge, east to Grand Canal Dock, and back. Flat, scenic, 30 minutes round trip. Perfect body-clock reset.

9:30 AM

Trinity College & Book of Kells

Student-led tour bundles campus walk with Book of Kells entry. Enter via Nassau Street for a quieter approach. Book now.

11:15 AM

Coffee at Kaph

Drury Street, 5-minute walk from Trinity. Excellent coffee, good pastries, quick stop. Gives the group a buffer before Guinness — no rushing.

Noon

Guinness Storehouse

Book noon entry + 1:30 PM lunch at the 1837 Brasserie as a combined package. Gravity Bar pint at the top, then lunch downstairs. Taxi from Kaph, ~8 minutes.

2:30 PM

Hotel rest

Essential block. 60–90 minutes. Everyone appreciates a midday reset before the afternoon.

4:00 PM

Irish Whiskey Museum (Premium tour)

3-minute walk from hotel. 4-whiskey comparative tasting, 60 minutes. Best whiskey option for this group.

Swap option: Hands-on whiskey blending

For a more intimate, curated experience, consider a hands-on blending session at a smaller Dublin distillery (see whiskey blending options). More personal, more memorable — you leave with your own bottle.

6:30 PM

Dinner at L. Mulligan Grocer

Stoneybatter, 10-minute walk from The Cobblestone. Gastropub with a serious kitchen — craft beer, whiskey-focused, quality well above pub standard. Locals-heavy, relaxed. ~20–30 EUR per main. Perfect pre-session fuel.

7:45 PM

Bar 1661 — poitín cocktails

Green Street, Smithfield — around the corner from The Cobblestone. A poitín-focused cocktail bar: Ireland’s original spirit, once illegal, now revived. Beautiful space, serious cocktails, fascinating story. The Dublin speakeasy equivalent. One or two rounds before the session.

9:00 PM

The Cobblestone trad session

Smithfield, no cover charge, musicians-in-the-round in the back room. Go at 9:00 for seats before the 9:30 session kicks off. Anyone who prefers a slower pace wraps up after dinner or after Bar 1661.

Swap option: The Pig’s Ear + Kehoe’s

If the group prefers to stay in the city center for Sunday evening: dinner at The Pig’s Ear (Nassau Street, overlooks Trinity, ~30–40 EUR mains, book 6:30 PM) followed by a pint at Kehoe’s (South Anne Street) — a genuine Dublin local with Victorian interior. Skip Bar 1661 and taxi to The Cobblestone at 9:00 if energy allows.

Monday

August 31 — Departure

8:00 AM

Leisurely breakfast, pack up

Check out of Trinity City.

9:30 AM

Kilmainham Gaol Strongly recommended — book 28 days ahead

The single most powerful historical experience in Dublin. The guided tour walks through the execution yard where the 1916 leaders were shot and the chapel where Joseph Plunkett married Grace Gifford hours before his execution. Set an alarm for August 2nd at midnight Irish time to book the 9:30 AM slot. Email kilmainhamgaol@opw.ie for the ground-floor accessible tour option.

11:00 AM

Collect the rental car

7-seat minivan, automatic. Pickup at Dublin Airport if doing Kilmainham, or city-center office otherwise.

Noon

Depart Dublin for Galway

~2.5 hours via the M6.
