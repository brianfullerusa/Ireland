Hatch & Sons

Return to Dublin activity review • Sunday 9/6 alternative brunch (primary: Cliff Townhouse)

Snapshot

A warm, no-fuss Irish food cafe housed in The Little Museum of Dublin on St. Stephen's Green. Hatch & Sons serves a beautiful Irish breakfast built on organic ingredients and farmhouse producers — proper sausages, free-range eggs, excellent brown bread baked in-house, and strong coffee. The setting is intimate: a handful of tables in a bright, simply decorated room that feels more like a friend's kitchen than a restaurant. It's not trying to be glamorous — it's trying to serve honest Irish food well, and it succeeds. Opens 9:30 AM on weekends.

Key facts

LocationThe Little Museum of Dublin, 15 St. Stephen’s Green, Dublin 2. Steps from the top of Grafton Street.
HoursOpens 9:30 AM on weekends. Closes mid-afternoon.
ReservationsNot always taken — arrive close to opening for the best chance of seating a group together.
PriceFull Irish breakfast ~12–16 EUR. Coffee, pastries, and lighter options also available.
CapacitySmall dining room — a party of seven may need to split across tables or wait for seating to free up.
AccessibilityGround-floor access. The space is small but navigable.

Try something different — alternative plans

If Hatch & Sons isn't the right farewell brunch, here are other options for Sunday morning:

⭐For the best-in-class breakfast

Cliff Townhouse

The panel's primary brunch pick. The Full Cliff breakfast with named Irish producers — Glenilen Farm eggs, Gubbeen chorizo, Clonakilty black pudding. More restaurant than cafe, slightly higher price point, but the ingredients speak for themselves. Call ahead for a party of seven.

🌆For Liffey views

The Woollen Mills

House-baked bread, excellent eggs, and views over the River Liffey. Opens 10:00 AM — later start. Run by the same group as The Winding Stair, so the food quality is reliable. A different part of Dublin for the final morning.

🌶For something different

Brother Hubbard

Middle Eastern-Irish fusion on Capel Street. Shakshuka, Turkish eggs, harissa. Popular with Dubliners, not tourists. Opens 9:30 AM weekends. The adventurous choice for the final breakfast.

Revisions agreed by the panel

  • Order the full Irish breakfast or eggs on toast — keep it simple.

Panel's final recommendation

Hatch & Sons is the panel's top backup if Cliff Townhouse can't seat the group. The food is honest, well-sourced, and satisfying — exactly what a departure-morning breakfast should be. The small room and lack of reservations are real concerns for a group of seven, but arriving at opening solves most of the problem. It's not as polished as Cliff Townhouse, but it's the same food philosophy in a more casual, faster-paced format.
