The Long Hall

Return to Dublin activity review • Saturday 9/5 alternative nightcap (primary: Kehoe's)

Snapshot

One of Dublin's most beautiful pub interiors, tucked behind a modest facade on South Great George's Street. The Long Hall dates to 1766 and retains its ornate Victorian fittings — carved mahogany, etched glass partitions, filigree ceiling work, and one of the longest bar counters in Dublin stretching the full depth of the narrow room. No music, no television, no food gimmicks — just proper pints, an excellent whiskey selection, and the kind of old-world atmosphere that makes you lower your voice and slow down. It's a pub for people who love pubs.

Key facts

Location51 South Great George’s Street, Dublin 2. 10-minute walk from St. Stephen’s Green.
HoursMonday–Thursday 4:00 PM–11:30 PM; Friday–Saturday 1:00 PM–12:30 AM; Sunday 1:00 PM–11:00 PM.
ReservationsNot taken — walk in. Arrive before 10:00 PM on Saturday for the best chance of finding a spot at the bar.
PricePint of Guinness ~6.50–7 EUR. Whiskey selection is extensive, with premium pours available.
Music/TVNone. No live music, no screens. Conversation only.
AccessibilityGround-floor entry. The interior is narrow and can be crowded on Saturday nights. Standing room predominates — limited seating.

Why we'd want to do this

  • The interior is genuinely stunning — one of the best-preserved Victorian pub rooms in Ireland.
  • The whiskey selection is excellent, making it ideal for a post-dinner dram.
  • No music, no TV, no distractions — just the sound of conversation and clinking glasses.
  • Walking distance from St. Stephen's Green and most city center restaurants.
  • A Saturday nightcap here is the kind of Dublin memory that sticks — the room feels timeless.

What's good to see

  • The bar itself — study the carved woodwork, the etched glass, the antique clock. Every detail is original.
  • The whiskey menu — ask the bartender for a recommendation. They know the collection.
  • The ceiling plasterwork and the ornamental gas lamp fittings (now electric, but original fixtures).
  • The atmosphere on a Saturday night — a mix of locals, visitors, and the occasional literary type.
  • Stand at the bar if possible — it's the best vantage point for appreciating the room's full length.

Common review themes

What reviewers love

  • "The most beautiful pub in Dublin" is a common refrain, and it's hard to argue.
  • The whiskey selection gets specific praise — well-curated and properly served.
  • The absence of music and screens is a feature, not a bug — reviewers appreciate the focus on conversation.
  • "Feels like stepping back in time" — the Victorian atmosphere is genuine, not reconstructed.

Common complaints

  • "Narrow and crowded" on weekend nights — the long, thin layout means it fills quickly.
  • Limited seating — most of the pub is standing room, especially at the bar.
  • "Hard to find a spot after 10 PM on Saturday" is a recurring note.
  • No food service — this is a drinking pub only.

How it compares to other options

Compared to Kehoe's, the primary nightcap recommendation:

Pros

  • More visually striking interior — the Victorian fittings are extraordinary.
  • Better whiskey selection for a post-dinner dram.
  • Slightly less crowded than Kehoe's on an average Saturday — though both get busy.
  • The atmosphere is grander — Kehoe's is intimate, The Long Hall is theatrical.

Cons & caveats

  • Less seating than Kehoe's — more standing room, which may not suit everyone after a long day.
  • The narrow layout can feel claustrophobic when packed.
  • Kehoe's has a warmer, more "local" feel — The Long Hall is slightly more of a showpiece.
  • Slightly further from St. Stephen's Green than Kehoe's (10 minutes vs. 5 minutes walk).
  • Both pubs are exceptional. Kehoe's is the cozy local; The Long Hall is the grand Victorian. Neither is wrong.
  • The choice often comes down to mood: intimate conversation (Kehoe's) vs. jaw-dropping interior (The Long Hall).
  • If the group can manage two stops, visiting both is entirely feasible — they're 8 minutes apart on foot.

Try something different — alternative plans

If The Long Hall isn't the right nightcap, here are other options for the last drink of the trip:

🍺For a cozy local

Kehoe's

The panel's primary nightcap pick. South Anne Street, 5 minutes from St. Stephen's Green. Victorian interior, no music, no TV — just pints and conversation. More intimate than The Long Hall, with better seating options. The classic Dublin pub experience.

🎵For a livelier night

O'Donoghue's

Merrion Row, near St. Stephen's Green. Famous as the pub where The Dubliners played their early sessions. Still hosts trad music most nights. More energy than Kehoe's or The Long Hall — the right choice if the group wants music with their last pint.

🌇For a hotel bar wind-down

The Shelbourne Bar

If the group is staying at or near The Shelbourne on St. Stephen's Green, the hotel bar is a dignified, no-effort nightcap. Comfortable seating, good whiskey selection, and zero logistics. Walk upstairs and go to bed.
