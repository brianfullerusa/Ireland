Jaunting Car Ride

Killarney activity review • Thursday 9/3 afternoon

Snapshot

The jaunting car is Killarney's most iconic experience — a traditional horse-drawn carriage ride through the lanes and lakeshores of Killarney National Park. The drivers, known as jarveys, are local characters who narrate the journey with a mix of history, folklore, humor, and gentle exaggeration that has been perfected over generations. Routes wind through the park past landmarks including Ross Castle, Muckross Abbey, and along the shores of the lakes, through ancient oak woodland and under mountain views that shift with the light. The jaunting car tradition in Killarney dates back to the early 19th century when the first tourists began arriving to see the lakes, and the jarveys have been an essential part of the Killarney experience ever since. Each car seats up to four passengers on a sideways-facing bench, with the jarvey perched up front.

Key facts

LocationJaunting cars can be picked up at several points: Killarney town center, Ross Castle, Muckross House, and Kate Kearney's Cottage (Gap of Dunloe).
RoutesMultiple routes available. The most popular runs from Muckross House through the park past Muckross Abbey and along the lakeshores. Duration varies by route: 30 minutes to 1.5 hours.
Cost~€40–€80 per car depending on route length. Each car seats up to 4 passengers. A group will need 2 cars.
BookingCan be arranged on the spot at pickup points, but pre-booking through your accommodation or a local operator is recommended for groups.
Duration30 minutes to 1.5 hours depending on route. The standard Muckross route is about 1 hour.
AccessibilityStepping up into the car requires some mobility. The bench seating is open-sided. Blankets are provided in cooler weather. The ride itself is gentle and comfortable on flat lanes.

Why it made the list

  • It's the quintessential Killarney experience — visitors have been taking jaunting car rides through the park since the 1800s.
  • The jarveys are genuinely entertaining — part guide, part comedian, part storyteller. Their narration brings the landscape to life.
  • The routes pass through some of the most beautiful scenery in the park — lakeshores, ancient woodland, mountain views, and historic ruins.
  • It's a relaxed afternoon activity that requires no physical effort — sit back and enjoy the ride.
  • A perfect complement to the morning's visit to Muckross House and the scenic drive to Ladies' View.

Common review themes

What reviewers love

  • The jarveys are universally praised — "our driver was hilarious," "the stories made the trip," "worth it for the commentary alone."
  • The pace is described as "magical" — slow enough to absorb the scenery, fast enough to cover real ground.
  • The routes through the park are consistently called "stunning" and "the highlight of Killarney."
  • Families and groups of all ages enjoy it — accessible fun that doesn't require hiking or driving.

Common complaints

  • Pricing can feel inconsistent — agree on the route and price before setting off.
  • Some jarveys are more engaging than others — quality of commentary varies.
  • Rain can dampen the experience (literally) — blankets and covers are provided but an umbrella helps.
  • Animal welfare concerns are occasionally raised — the horses are regulated and inspected, but some visitors are uncomfortable with the concept.

Try something different — alternative plans

If a jaunting car ride doesn't appeal, alternatives for Thursday afternoon:

🚶For a walk instead

Muckross Abbey and lakeside walk

Walk through the park on foot instead. The path from Muckross House to the abbey and along the lakeshore is flat, paved, and beautiful. Free, self-paced, and as long or short as you like.

🗺For more scenery

Extended drive — Moll's Gap and Kenmare

Continue the morning's scenic drive south through Moll's Gap to the colorful town of Kenmare. Browse the shops, have coffee, and return via a different route. About 2 hours round trip.

Round 1 — Opening views

Ireland Specialist
You can't come to Killarney and not take a jaunting car. It's been part of the Killarney experience for over 200 years. The jarveys are characters — they'll tell you stories about the lakes, the mountains, the local families, and they'll make half of it up with a straight face. It's wonderful.
Group Logistics
For this group, you'll need two cars. Each seats four comfortably. I'd recommend booking in advance through your hotel or a local operator rather than negotiating at a pickup point. Agree on the route and price beforehand so there are no surprises.
Accessibility Advisor
Getting into the car requires stepping up to a bench that sits about two feet off the ground. There's usually a step and a handle. Once seated, the ride is smooth and comfortable on the flat park lanes. Blankets are provided. The jarvey can help with boarding.
Food & Culture Lead
This is a cultural experience as much as a scenic one. The jarvey tradition is a living piece of Killarney's heritage. Listen to the stories, ask questions, and enjoy the pace. It's the opposite of rushing through sights in a car.

Round 2 — Back-and-forth

Ireland Specialist
The best route for a group is from Muckross House through the park. You pass Muckross Abbey, go along the lakeshore, through the woodland, and get dropped back at the house or continue to Ross Castle. About an hour. It's the classic route.
Group Logistics
Timing-wise, this fits perfectly after the morning at Muckross House and the scenic drive. Return to the hotel for a rest, then do the jaunting car ride at 3:00 or 3:30 PM. Back in time for dinner at Treyvaud's.
Food & Culture Lead
Tip the jarvey — €5–€10 per car is appropriate if you enjoyed the ride. They work hard and the good ones are genuinely talented storytellers.
Accessibility Advisor
If anyone in the group prefers not to ride, the walking path follows much of the same route. They can walk at their own pace and meet the group at the endpoint. The paths are flat and paved throughout the park.

The jaunting car ride is an essential Killarney experience. It combines stunning National Park scenery with the unique entertainment of a jarvey's narration — a tradition that has been part of Killarney tourism for over two centuries. Book two cars for the group, take the Muckross route through the park on Thursday afternoon, and enjoy a leisurely hour of lakeside views and local storytelling.
