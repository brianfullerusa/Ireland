Brother Hubbard

Return to Dublin activity review • Sunday 9/6 alternative brunch (primary: Cliff Townhouse)

Snapshot

A Middle Eastern-Irish fusion cafe on Capel Street, one of Dublin's most vibrant north-side streets. Brother Hubbard has built a loyal following among Dubliners for its distinctive brunch menu — shakshuka, Turkish eggs (cilbir), harissa-spiced dishes, and excellent flat whites. The cooking draws from North African and Middle Eastern traditions while using Irish ingredients, creating a flavor profile unlike anything else on the brunch list. The cafe is bright and energetic, popular with locals rather than tourists, and opens at 9:30 AM on weekends. It's the adventurous choice for the final breakfast of the trip.

Key facts

Location153 Capel Street, Dublin 1. North side of the Liffey, 15-minute walk from St. Stephen’s Green.
HoursOpens 9:30 AM on weekends. Brunch served until early afternoon.
ReservationsNot always taken for brunch. Arrive close to 9:30 AM opening for the best chance of seating.
PriceBrunch dishes ~12–18 EUR. Coffee is excellent (flat whites a specialty).
StyleMiddle Eastern-Irish fusion. Shakshuka, Turkish eggs, harissa, spiced dishes with Irish ingredients.
AccessibilityGround-floor entry. The cafe can be busy and the layout is compact but manageable.

Why we'd want to do this

  • A completely different flavor profile from every other meal on the trip — Middle Eastern spices, harissa, yogurt, and chili butter.
  • Popular with Dubliners, not tourists — the clientele is local, the atmosphere is genuine.
  • Capel Street is one of Dublin's most interesting neighborhoods — a vibrant, multicultural stretch that shows a different side of the city.
  • The shakshuka and Turkish eggs are destination dishes — people cross Dublin for them.
  • Opens at 9:30 AM, giving a workable departure-morning schedule.

What's good to see

  • The shakshuka — eggs baked in a spiced tomato and pepper sauce, served in the pan with bread for dipping.
  • Turkish eggs (cilbir) — poached eggs on yogurt with chili butter and sourdough. A signature dish.
  • The harissa-spiced sausage roll or other pastries from the counter.
  • Flat whites — the coffee program is taken seriously and the flat whites are consistently excellent.
  • Capel Street itself — walk the block before or after brunch to see the murals, independent shops, and neighborhood energy.

Try something different — alternative plans

If Brother Hubbard isn't the right farewell brunch, here are other options for Sunday morning:

⭐For the best Irish breakfast

Cliff Townhouse

The panel's primary brunch pick on St. Stephen's Green. The Full Cliff breakfast with Glenilen Farm eggs, Gubbeen chorizo, and Clonakilty black pudding. The definitive Irish farewell breakfast. Call ahead for a party of seven.

🍴For a casual Irish breakfast

Hatch & Sons

Organic Irish breakfast on St. Stephen's Green. Farmhouse sausages, proper brown bread, strong coffee. Opens 9:30 AM. Simpler and more casual than Cliff Townhouse, with the same honest approach to ingredients.

🌆For Liffey views

The Woollen Mills

House-baked bread, excellent eggs, and views over the Ha'penny Bridge. Opens 10:00 AM Sundays. The setting pick — the food is reliable and the view is unbeatable. Slightly tighter departure-morning schedule.

Round 1 — Opening views

Brother Hubbard is modern Dublin on a plate. Capel Street has transformed in the last decade — it's now one of the most vibrant, multicultural streets in the city. Having the last breakfast here shows a side of Dublin that most visitors never encounter.

The concern is group consensus. After a week of Irish food, some may relish the change; others may want their last meal in Ireland to be a proper Irish breakfast. This pick works best if the whole group is excited about it — it's not a compromise choice.

Ireland Specialist

Revisions agreed by the panel

  • Only choose Brother Hubbard if the group is genuinely enthusiastic about the Middle Eastern-Irish fusion concept.
  • Arrive at 9:30 AM opening to avoid the weekend queue.
  • Order the shakshuka or Turkish eggs — they're the reason to come. Simpler options are available for those who prefer them.
  • Check out of the hotel first and head north for brunch. The drive to the airport from Capel Street is shorter (~20 minutes).
  • Budget 45–60 minutes for the meal — fast, efficient service.

Panel's final recommendation

Brother Hubbard is the wild card on the brunch list — the right choice for a group that wants something unexpected and delicious for the final meal of the trip. The shakshuka and Turkish eggs are destination dishes, the coffee is excellent, and Capel Street offers a side of Dublin most visitors never see. It's not the "farewell Irish breakfast" narrative, but it's a memorable meal in its own right. Choose it if the group is adventurous; default to Cliff Townhouse if the traditional farewell breakfast matters more.
