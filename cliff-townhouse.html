Sun 9/6 brunch #04

Cliff Townhouse

Return to Dublin activity review • Sunday 9/6 brunch before the airport

Snapshot

A boutique hotel and restaurant on St. Stephen's Green North, famous for what may be the best breakfast in Dublin. "The Full Cliff" is the signature: Glenilen Farm eggs, Gubbeen chorizo, Jack McCarthy sausages, Clonakilty black pudding, proper brown bread, and excellent coffee. Every ingredient on the plate has a named Irish producer behind it — this isn't a hotel breakfast, it's a statement about Irish food culture. The dining room is elegant but relaxed, occupying the ground floor of a Georgian townhouse overlooking the Green. The kind of meal that makes the last bite of the trip count.

Why we'd want to do this

  • The Full Cliff may be the single best Irish breakfast in Dublin — every ingredient is sourced with care and intention.
  • The named-producer approach means every bite tells a story: Glenilen from West Cork, Gubbeen from the Beara Peninsula, Clonakilty from the same town the group may have passed through.
  • Location on St. Stephen's Green means it's walkable from any city center hotel and perfectly positioned after the Grand Canal Loop walk.
  • A proper farewell meal — the last food in Ireland should be the best Irish breakfast available.
  • The Georgian dining room is a beautiful setting for a leisurely Sunday morning.

What's good to see

  • The Full Cliff is the headliner — order it and let the plate speak for itself.
  • The eggs Benedict with Gubbeen chorizo is an excellent alternative for anyone who doesn't want the full Irish.
  • The brown bread is baked in-house and served warm — it's a meal in itself with good Irish butter.
  • Coffee is properly made and generously refilled.
  • The dining room's Georgian proportions — high ceilings, tall windows, natural light flooding in from the Green.

Common review themes

What reviewers love

Common complaints

  • Some reviewers note the hotel lobby entrance can be confusing — the restaurant is through the lobby on the ground floor.

Try something different — alternative plans

If Cliff Townhouse isn't the right farewell brunch, here are other options for Sunday morning:

🍴For a casual Irish breakfast

Hatch & Sons

Organic Irish breakfast at The Little Museum of Dublin on St. Stephen's Green. Farmhouse sausages, proper brown bread, strong coffee. Opens 9:30 AM weekends. A reliable, no-fuss alternative just steps away from Cliff Townhouse.

🌆For Liffey views

The Woollen Mills

House-baked bread, excellent eggs, and views over the River Liffey from Ormond Quay. Opens 10:00 AM Sundays — a slightly later start that suits anyone who stayed out late at Kehoe's. Bright, airy space with a relaxed weekend atmosphere.

🌶For something different

Brother Hubbard

Middle Eastern-Irish fusion on Capel Street. Shakshuka, Turkish eggs, harissa, and excellent flat whites. A local favorite that opens at 9:30 AM. A different flavor profile for the final breakfast — the right choice for adventurous palates.
