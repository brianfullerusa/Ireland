The Brazen Head (Pub Dinner + Trad Music)

Dublin activity review • Saturday 8/29 alternative dinner (primary: The Winding Stair)

Snapshot

Claims the title of Ireland’s oldest pub — a coaching-inn site continuously operating since 1198. A warren of low-ceilinged rooms, uneven floors, and candlelit snugs on Bridge Street, 12 minutes west of Trinity City. Classic Irish pub menu (stew, fish & chips, Sunday roast) and reliable traditional music sessions nightly in the summer, starting around 9:30 PM. The tourist quotient is high, but so is the atmosphere.

Gallery — what you might see

Key facts

Location20 Bridge Street Lower. 12-min walk or 5-min taxi from Trinity City.
HoursDaily 10:30–late. Kitchen serves lunch and dinner; food service until ~22:00.
MusicNightly trad sessions in summer, usually starting 21:30. Also storytelling dinners on select nights.
ReservationsBook dinner ahead via opentable.com — essential for weekends.
PriceMains ~€20–28. Pint of Guinness ~€6.50–7.
AccessibilityLimited. Historic building, uneven floors, narrow doorways, no lift. Main dining area is ground-floor but crowded.

Why we’d want to do this

  • Arrival-night first Dublin pub should feel iconic — this delivers more atmosphere than anywhere else.
  • One-stop shop: dinner + trad music + Guinness in historic surroundings.
  • The claim to being “oldest pub in Ireland” (disputed, but unchallenged here) makes it a bragging-rights stop.
  • Good for a jet-lagged arrival dinner — food is solid, booking holds your table, and you don’t need to wander looking for a spot.
  • Families with Irish ancestry often want that “first pint in an old Dublin pub” photo.

What’s good to see

  • The Irish storytelling dinner (separate ticket, different nights) — seanachí tradition, surprisingly engaging.
  • The main dining room’s low ceilings and centuries-old stonework.
  • The exterior courtyard with heated canopy — often has overflow music in summer.
  • Classic menu staples: Irish stew, bangers and mash, beef & Guinness pie.
  • Live trad music 6 nights a week in summer.

Common review themes

What reviewers love

  • “Atmosphere is everything here” is a common positive note.
  • First-time Dublin visitors almost always rate it highly — it matches the idea of an “Irish pub” in your head.
  • The storytelling dinner gets specific praise — unique offering.
  • Music sessions on weekends are strong.

Common complaints

  • “Food is inconsistent” and “overpriced” are the two most common complaints.
  • “Touristy” — locals rarely go here. The clientele is 95% visitors.
  • Service quality varies significantly by night and staff.
  • Accessibility issues — tight spaces, uneven floors, crowded at peak times.
  • Music timing: sessions don’t start until 21:30, which is past most jet-lag bedtimes.

How it compares to other options

Compared to other Dublin dinner/pub options:

Pros

  • Best “first night in Dublin” atmosphere of any pub.
  • Dinner + pub + music under one roof.
  • Easy to book and hold a table for a larger group.
  • Iconic status makes it a memorable story.

Cons & caveats

  • Food and service are inconsistent.
  • Definitively touristy.
  • Accessibility challenges — uneven floors, tight spaces.
  • Music doesn’t start until 21:30 — jet-lagged group likely gone by then.
  • Pure trad music quality is better at The Cobblestone or O’Donoghue’s.
  • Food quality is better at The Winding Stair (primary Saturday dinner) or L. Mulligan Grocer.
  • Brazen Head is best when you value atmosphere + one-stop convenience over best-in-class music or food.

Try something different — alternative plans

If Brazen Head isn't the right first-night dinner, alternatives for Saturday evening:

⛳For the golf fans

Portmarnock Hotel Links Brasserie

If you're choosing a different first-night dinner, the Links Brasserie at Portmarnock Hotel has the Atlantic and dunes as a backdrop. 30-minute taxi from Trinity City, but worth it for a memorable first night in Ireland. No tee-time commitment needed to dine. portmarnock.com.

⚽For the sports fans

The Camden — proper sports pub

Sports pub on Camden Street with every channel — Saturday night Premier League, GAA highlights, and rugby on multiple screens. Food is solid pub fare (burgers, fish & chips, stew). If a specific match is must-watch for anyone, this is the spot. Easy 10-minute taxi from Trinity City.

🗺For the sightseeing fans

Winding Stair restaurant + Ha'penny Bridge stroll

First-floor dining room overlooking the River Liffey and the Ha'penny Bridge. Modern Irish menu with strong Irish sourcing, excellent reputation. 2-minute walk across the bridge to the Temple Bar side after. A pre-dinner stroll along the quays is magical at dusk. Book winding-stair.com.

Panel’s final recommendation

It’s not the best food or the best music in Dublin, but it’s the best first-night-in-Dublin atmosphere. For Sunday evening, dinner is at L.
