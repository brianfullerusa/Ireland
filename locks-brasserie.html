Locks Brasserie

Return to Dublin activity review • Saturday 9/5 alternative farewell dinner (primary: Chapter One)

Snapshot

A French-Irish brasserie perched on the banks of the Grand Canal at the third lock in Portobello, one of Dublin's most appealing residential neighborhoods. The cooking is classic brasserie — steak frites, fish of the day, seasonal salads, proper desserts — executed with care and served in a bright, relaxed room with views over the canal. In summer, the terrace overlooking the lock is one of Dublin's loveliest outdoor dining spots. The atmosphere says neighborhood restaurant rather than destination dining, and that's part of its charm.

Key facts

Location1 Windsor Terrace, Portobello, Dublin 8. 15-minute walk or 8-minute taxi from St. Stephen’s Green.
HoursDinner: Wednesday–Saturday from 5:30 PM. Sunday lunch also available.
ReservationsRecommended for Saturday evening. Book via locksbrasserie.com or call directly.
PriceMains ~30–40 EUR. Wine list is well-curated with good value French and Irish options.
TerraceOutdoor terrace overlooking the Grand Canal lock. Available in dry weather — September evenings are a toss-up.
AccessibilityGround-floor dining room. The terrace is step-free. More accessible than the basement alternatives.

Why we'd want to do this

  • The most relaxed farewell dinner option — brasserie style means no pressure to dress up or perform.
  • Canal-side setting with a terrace that catches the evening light beautifully in early September.
  • Classic, crowd-pleasing cooking — steak frites, fresh fish, proper desserts — everyone finds something they want.
  • Portobello is a charming neighborhood that most visitors never see — it's a local's Dublin experience.
  • Ground-floor access makes it the most accessible fine-dining alternative on the list.

What's good to see

  • The steak frites with bearnaise is the signature — perfectly executed brasserie cooking.
  • Fish of the day depends on what the boats brought in — always worth asking about.
  • The dessert menu takes itself seriously: tarte tatin, creme brulee, chocolate fondant.
  • The canal walk to and from the restaurant is lovely in the evening light.
  • The wine list favors French producers and has good options by the glass.

Common review themes

What reviewers love

  • "Feels like a neighborhood restaurant in Paris" is the recurring compliment.
  • The terrace is universally praised when weather cooperates.
  • Consistent quality — reviewers who return multiple times report reliably good meals.
  • Service is described as "friendly and professional" with a personal touch.

Common complaints

  • The Portobello location is a 15-minute walk or short taxi from the city center — slightly out of the way.
  • The terrace is weather-dependent and September evenings can be cool.
  • Not the place for adventurous or avant-garde cooking — it's brasserie classics, not innovation.
  • Can feel quiet on weeknights — Saturday is the best night for atmosphere.

Try something different — alternative plans

If Locks Brasserie isn't the right farewell dinner, here are other strong options for Saturday evening:

⭐For the top-tier experience

Chapter One by Mickael Viljanen

Michelin-starred modern European in the vaulted basement of the Dublin Writers Museum. The panel's primary pick for the farewell dinner. Tasting menu ~85 EUR. Book well in advance.

🍷For an intimate evening

Dax Restaurant

French-Irish fine dining in a Georgian basement on Pembroke Street. Classical technique, strong wine list, walking distance from St. Stephen's Green. A refined, quieter alternative.

🍴For a relaxed evening

The Winding Stair

Modern Irish restaurant overlooking the Ha'penny Bridge. Daily-changing menu with named Irish producers and a warm, lively room. More casual than Locks, with a distinctly Irish menu. ~35–45 EUR mains.

Panel's final recommendation

Locks Brasserie is the panel's pick for a relaxed, accessible farewell dinner. The canal-side setting, reliable brasserie cooking, and neighborhood atmosphere make it a warm, low-pressure way to close the trip. It lacks the occasion-level prestige of Chapter One but compensates with charm, value, and ease. Choose Locks if the group wants a dinner that feels like a celebration rather than a performance.
