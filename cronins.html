Cronin's Restaurant

Killarney activity review • Alternative to The Cellar for Friday evening

Snapshot

Cronin's is a reliable, family-run restaurant in Killarney that serves as a dependable alternative to The Cellar for the final Friday evening dinner. It offers a more relaxed, less formal atmosphere than the hotel cellar setting, with a menu built around solid Irish cooking — steaks, seafood, traditional dishes, and a good selection of desserts. Cronin's is the kind of restaurant where locals eat regularly and visitors feel immediately welcome. The service is warm and efficient, the portions are generous, and the prices are fair. It may lack the architectural drama of The Cellar's vaulted basement, but it makes up for it with unpretentious comfort and consistently good food.

Why it made the list

  • A reliable, no-surprises dinner option for the final evening — consistent quality without pretension.
  • More accessible than The Cellar — ground-floor dining without the stairs of a basement venue.
  • Lower price point than The Cellar or Treyvaud's, which may appeal after several restaurant dinners.
  • Family-run warmth — the kind of place that treats every table like guests, not customers.
  • A quieter, more relaxed atmosphere for a group that's had a long day driving the Dingle Peninsula.

  • May feel anticlimactic as a "final night" dinner if the group wants something more special.
