Teeling Whiskey Distillery

Dublin activity review • Sunday 8/30 afternoon option B (whiskey)

Snapshot

Teeling is the first new distillery to open in Dublin in over 125 years (2015), and the only currently operating whiskey distillery inside the city. Located in the Liberties neighborhood near the Guinness Storehouse, it was founded by Jack and Stephen Teeling from a family with 250 years of Dublin whiskey heritage. You watch real production — mash tuns, copper pot stills, active fermentation — not a branded recreation.

Key facts

Location13–17 Newmarket, Liberties. ~10 min walk from Guinness Storehouse, ~15 min taxi from Trinity City
HoursMon–Sat 10:00–18:00, Sun 11:00–18:00. Last tour ~17:00.
TicketsStandard tour ~€25, Premium (3 whiskeys) ~€40, The Reserve tour ~€60+. Book ahead.
Duration60 min for standard tour incl. tasting; 90 min for premium
AccessibilityDecent but not perfect. Main tour route has a few short steps; call ahead to arrange an alternative route if anyone wants to avoid steps.
Key advantageYou see actual whiskey being made. The only in-Dublin distillery where this is true.

Why we’d want to do this

  • The only working distillery tour in Dublin — you hear, see, and smell live production.
  • Smaller, more personal group sizes than Jameson (typically 12–18 per tour).
  • Guides are actual distillery staff, often including family members — more authentic feel.
  • Pairs geographically with Guinness (10 min walk apart) — no extra taxis across the city.
  • For whiskey enthusiasts, this is the Dublin stop to prioritize.

What’s good to see

  • The three copper pot stills — Alison, Natalie, and Rebecca — named after the Teeling daughters.
  • Walking past active mash tuns with the smell of warm grain (genuinely memorable).
  • The tasting room with its signature Small Batch rum-cask-finished whiskey.
  • The Phoenix statue at the entrance — symbol of Dublin distilling’s resurrection.
  • On-site bar serves rare releases and whiskey cocktails.

Common review themes

What reviewers love

  • “Better than Jameson” is a recurring review headline — reviewers love seeing actual production.
  • Guides are praised as knowledgeable, passionate, and less scripted than at Jameson.
  • Smaller tour groups consistently get positive reviews.
  • Whiskey enthusiasts rate the tasting as more sophisticated than Jameson’s — more challenging flavors, less “easy crowd-pleaser” profile.

Common complaints

  • “The tasting was nowhere near as good as Jameson’s” is a minority but persistent view — it’s a matter of taste.
  • Accessibility is more limited than Jameson. Multiple reviewers note steps on the standard tour.
  • Neighborhood (Liberties) is atmospheric but grittier — some older visitors feel less comfortable walking to it.
  • Smaller gift shop, limited merchandise vs. Jameson.

How it compares to other options

Head-to-head with the other whiskey options:

Pros

  • Authentic working-distillery experience — unique in Dublin.
  • Smaller, more personal tour group.
  • Geographically close to Guinness — logistics are clean.
  • Better for whiskey enthusiasts.

Cons & caveats

  • Accessibility considerations — confirm step-free route in advance.
  • Neighborhood is less central/polished than Jameson’s.
  • Tasting style is more challenging; not as “crowd-pleasing.”
  • For a whiskey-enthusiast subset of the group, Teeling is the pick.
  • If anyone in the group wants to avoid stairs: call Teeling ahead for the accessible tour route, or default to Jameson/Irish Whiskey Museum.

Panel’s final recommendation
