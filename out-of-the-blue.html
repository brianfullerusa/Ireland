Out of the Blue

Killarney activity review • Friday 9/4 lunch in Dingle

Snapshot

Out of the Blue is a legendary seafood restaurant on Strand Street in Dingle, housed in a distinctive blue-painted building right on the waterfront overlooking Dingle Harbour. The restaurant operates on a singular principle: it serves only fish and seafood caught that day from local boats. There is no meat on the menu. If the fishing boats don't go out or the catch is poor, the restaurant simply doesn't open — a policy that sounds dramatic but speaks to the uncompromising commitment to freshness that has made Out of the Blue one of the most acclaimed seafood restaurants in Ireland. The menu changes daily based on what comes off the boats, and the cooking lets the quality of the ingredients speak for itself. It has won numerous food awards and regularly appears on lists of Ireland's best restaurants.

Key facts

LocationStrand Street, Dingle waterfront. The distinctive blue building is impossible to miss.
HoursLunch from 12:30 PM, dinner from 6:00 PM. Closed Tuesdays. May close without notice if no fresh fish is available.
Price range€18–€35 per main course at lunch. Full lunch with drinks approximately €35–€55 per person.
ReservationsRecommended but not always required for lunch. Dinner reservations essential. Walk-ins possible at lunch if you arrive early.
MenuSeafood only — no meat dishes. Menu changes daily based on the catch. Expect crab, lobster, monkfish, hake, sea bass, and whatever else the boats bring in.
AccessibilityGround-floor restaurant. Confirm entrance accessibility in advance.

Why it made the list

  • One of Ireland's most acclaimed seafood restaurants — the "no fish, no open" policy is legendary and guarantees freshness.
  • The daily-changing menu means every visit is unique — you eat what the sea provides.
  • The waterfront location in Dingle adds to the experience — you can see the harbour where the fish was landed that morning.
  • The cooking is confident and unfussy — it lets extraordinary ingredients speak for themselves.
  • A perfect lunch stop during the Dingle Peninsula day — the highlight of the food calendar for the entire trip.

Common review themes

What reviewers love

  • "The freshest seafood I've ever eaten" is the most common review theme — the boat-to-table freshness is evident in every bite.
  • The daily menu creates excitement — reviewers enjoy not knowing exactly what will be available.
  • The cooking is praised as "simple but perfect" — excellent ingredients prepared with skill and restraint.
  • The blue building and harbour setting add atmosphere that enhances the meal.

Common complaints

  • No meat options at all — anyone who doesn't eat seafood will need an alternative plan.
  • The restaurant may not open on any given day — have a backup in mind.
  • Prices are higher than average Dingle restaurants, though most reviewers say it's justified.
  • Can be busy at peak lunch times — arrive by 12:30 or be prepared for a wait.

Try something different — alternative plans

If Out of the Blue is closed or doesn't suit everyone in the group:

🍴For variety beyond seafood

Dingle town — multiple options

Dingle has excellent restaurants beyond Out of the Blue. The group can split and explore — Murphy's Ice Cream for dessert, fish and chips on the pier, or one of the many cafes on Main Street. Dingle doesn't have a bad meal.

🍴For a pub lunch

Dick Mack's or another Dingle pub

Several Dingle pubs serve excellent food. A pub lunch is more casual, often cheaper, and gives the group flexibility to eat at their own pace while exploring the town.
