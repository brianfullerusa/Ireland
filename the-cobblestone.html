The Cobblestone — Traditional Music Session

Dublin activity review • Sunday 8/30 evening alternative

Snapshot

A plain-spoken public house in Smithfield run by the Mulligan family for four generations. Widely regarded by musicians and trad-music fans as the single best trad session pub in Dublin. No amplification, no “show” — players sit in a corner, pints are served over a narrow bar, and serious traditional music happens nightly. Survived a near-demolition in 2021 thanks to a public preservation campaign.

Key facts

Location77 King Street North, Smithfield. 10-min taxi from Trinity City.
HoursDaily from ~16:00 or 17:00 (varies). Closes ~23:30–00:30.
Music scheduleTrad sessions nightly, usually starting 19:00 or 19:30. Sundays often have earlier afternoon sessions.
ReservationsNo reservations — first come, first served. Arrive by 18:30 for Sunday session.
PricePint of Guinness ~€6.50. No food served — eat beforehand.
AccessibilityGround-floor, step-free entry. Pub is small; once seated the parents will be fine. No lift, no accessible restroom — confirm comfort in advance.

Why we’d want to do this

  • Best authentic trad music session pub in Dublin — musicians’ choice.
  • No amplification, no tourist-trap performance — you hear real Irish music as locals have for generations.
  • Less touristy than Brazen Head or O’Donoghue’s.
  • Sunday sessions often include “session of the week” caliber players.
  • Pairs naturally with an afternoon Smithfield whiskey stop (Jameson, Teeling) — cleans up the taxi arc.

What’s good to see

  • The back room where the musicians sit — closest you’ll get to a real session without being a player yourself.
  • The front bar where locals drink — atmosphere alone is worth the visit.
  • The 2021 preservation story: a proposed hotel demolition was blocked by public outcry. The pub now has a plaque.
  • Frequent appearances by nationally-known trad musicians dropping in for a pint and a tune.
  • Step dancers occasionally join sessions — spontaneous, not scheduled.

Common review themes

What reviewers love

  • “The real thing” is the most common review phrase.
  • Musicians rate it as the top trad pub in Ireland, not just Dublin.
  • “You feel like a guest in someone’s living room” — the opposite of touristy.
  • Staff are friendly to respectful visitors; no resentment toward tourists who behave well.

Common complaints

  • Small — gets crammed in peak hours. Standing-room-only after 20:00 on weekends.
  • No food — must eat beforehand. No menu, no snacks.
  • Restrooms are basic.
  • If you want a big show, this is not that. The music is for the musicians’ sake, not the audience’s.
  • Some Sunday sessions are “closed” (invited players only) and only partially open to the public.

How it compares to other options

Head-to-head with the other trad music options:

Pros

  • Most authentic trad music in Dublin.
  • Less touristy than any alternative.
  • Step-free ground-floor access.
  • Smithfield location pairs with a daytime whiskey stop.

Cons & caveats

  • No reservations, arrive early.
  • No food.
  • Small venue, crowds get tight.
  • Not a “performance” — if the group expects a show, they’ll be confused.
  • If the priority is music quality and authenticity, this wins.
  • If the priority is dining + music under one roof, Brazen Head wins.
  • If the priority is central location, O’Donoghue’s wins.
