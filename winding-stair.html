The Winding Stair

Dublin activity review • Saturday 8/29 primary dinner

Snapshot

A modern Irish restaurant on Ormond Quay Lower, perched above the famous Winding Stair Bookshop and overlooking the Ha'penny Bridge and the River Liffey. The menu changes daily, built around ingredients from named Irish farms and producers — Fingal Ferguson's gubbeen, Andarl Farm eggs, Kilmore Quay fish. It's refined without being fussy, literary without being pretentious, and consistently one of Dublin's most beloved restaurants since reopening in 2006. Two floors of warm, book-lined dining rooms with views that make you want to linger.

What it is

The Winding Stair occupies the first floor above one of Dublin's most storied independent bookshops. The bookshop opened in 1978 and became a gathering place for writers, musicians, and artists during some of Dublin's hardest decades. When the restaurant opened above it in 2006, it inherited that literary spirit — bookshelves line the walls, the atmosphere is warm and unhurried, and the cooking is rooted in the land.

The menu changes daily based on what arrives from a network of small Irish producers. You might find pan-fried hake from Kilmore Quay, slow-braised pork shoulder with colcannon, Cashel Blue cheese with walnut bread, or a farmhouse cheese board that reads like a map of rural Ireland. The kitchen's philosophy is simple: source brilliantly, cook honestly, let the ingredients speak.

The dining room looks directly out over the Ha'penny Bridge and the Liffey. At dusk on a summer evening, with the light on the river and a glass of something good, it's one of the best seats in Dublin.

Why it made the list

  • Consistently rated among Dublin's best restaurants for quality of ingredients and cooking.
  • The Ha'penny Bridge view from the dining room is genuinely special — not a gimmick, but a real bonus.
  • Daily-changing menus mean you're eating what's freshest and best that day.
  • Named producers on the menu create a direct connection to Irish food culture and farming traditions.
  • The bookshop downstairs is worth browsing before or after dinner — Irish literature, poetry, and art.
  • Warm, unpretentious atmosphere that feels distinctly Dublin without being touristy.

Common review themes

What reviewers love

  • "Best meal we had in Dublin" is a recurring theme across reviews.
  • The view over the Ha'penny Bridge is consistently praised as magical at sunset.
  • Staff are described as knowledgeable and genuinely passionate about the food.
  • The connection to named Irish farms and producers resonates with visitors.
  • The bookshop-restaurant combination feels unique and characterful.

Common complaints

  • The staircase is a barrier for anyone with mobility issues — no lift.
  • Tables can feel close together, especially on busy Saturday nights.
  • Some reviewers find the prices high for portion sizes.
  • The daily-changing menu means you can't guarantee a specific dish will be available.
  • Booking well in advance is essential — walk-ins are rarely successful on weekends.

Try something different — alternative plans

If The Winding Stair isn't the right Saturday dinner, consider these alternatives:

🍺Classic pub atmosphere

The Brazen Head — pub dinner + trad music

Ireland's oldest pub (1198). Less refined food, but unbeatable first-night atmosphere with trad music sessions starting at 9:30 PM. Book 6:30 PM, order safe classics like Irish stew or beef & Guinness pie.

🍽Stay near the hotel

The Pig's Ear — modern Irish on Nassau Street

Overlooking Trinity College, just minutes from the hotel. Similar quality to The Winding Stair but south of the Liffey. Elevator-accessible upstairs dining room. Good wine list, refined but unpretentious cooking.

Winding Stair at 7:00 PM → Stag's Head by 9:30
