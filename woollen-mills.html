The Woollen Mills

Return to Dublin activity review • Sunday 9/6 alternative brunch (primary: Cliff Townhouse)

Snapshot

A bright, airy restaurant and cafe on Ormond Quay Lower, directly overlooking the River Liffey and the Ha'penny Bridge. Run by the same group behind The Winding Stair next door, The Woollen Mills shares that restaurant's commitment to quality ingredients and house-baked bread. The building was originally a woollen mill and fabric shop — the high ceilings, large windows, and industrial character remain. Excellent eggs, house-baked bread, great coffee, and one of the best views in Dublin for a Sunday morning. Opens 10:00 AM on Sundays, which suits anyone who stayed out late the night before.

Why we'd want to do this

  • The Liffey views from the dining room are among the best in Dublin — the Ha'penny Bridge framed in the window is a memorable last-morning scene.
  • Run by the same team as The Winding Stair, guaranteeing quality ingredients and excellent house-baked bread.
  • The 10:00 AM Sunday opening suits anyone who had a late night at Kehoe's or The Long Hall.
  • The bright, spacious interior with high ceilings is a comfortable, relaxed setting for a group brunch.
  • A different part of Dublin than St. Stephen's Green — the quays have their own character.

What's good to see

  • The house-baked bread — same bakers as The Winding Stair, and it's excellent.
  • Eggs any way you want them — poached, scrambled, or as part of a full Irish.
  • The window tables overlooking the Liffey — request one when you arrive.
  • The coffee is well-made and generously poured.
  • The building itself — original timber beams, high ceilings, and a sense of Dublin's industrial heritage.

If The Woollen Mills isn't the right farewell brunch, here are other options for Sunday morning:

Cliff Townhouse

Organic Irish breakfast on St. Stephen's Green. Farmhouse sausages, proper brown bread. Opens 9:30 AM. Smaller room but same food philosophy as Cliff Townhouse in a more casual format.

Middle Eastern-Irish fusion on Capel Street. Shakshuka, Turkish eggs, harissa. Also north-side, opens 9:30 AM. A completely different flavor for the final breakfast.

Revisions agreed by the panel

  • Consider checking out of the hotel before brunch to remove the time pressure of the 10:00 AM opening.
  • Arrive at 10:00 AM opening. Request a window table for Liffey views.
  • Order house-baked bread, eggs, and coffee — keep it simple and fast.
  • The walk or taxi from St. Stephen's Green to Ormond Quay takes ~15 minutes. Factor that into the morning schedule.
  • From The Woollen Mills, the drive to Dublin Airport is ~20–25 minutes — straightforward.

Panel's final recommendation

The Woollen Mills is the right brunch alternative if the view matters — nothing else on the list competes with the Liffey and Ha'penny Bridge from a window table. The 10:00 AM Sunday opening creates a tighter schedule, but checking out of the hotel first solves the timing. The food is reliably good, the space is comfortable for a group, and the connection to The Winding Stair kitchen guarantees quality. Choose it for the setting; stay for the bread.
