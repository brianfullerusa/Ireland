Sat 9/5 dinner #01

Chapter One by Mickael Viljanen

Return to Dublin activity review • Saturday 9/5 farewell dinner

Snapshot

Michelin-starred modern European restaurant housed in the vaulted brick basement of the Dublin Writers Museum on Parnell Square North. Chef Mickael Viljanen brings exacting French technique to the finest Irish seasonal produce — the result is serious, memorable cooking served in a room with genuine architectural character. Warm lighting, exposed brick arches, and an atmosphere that feels celebratory without being stiff. The tasting menu runs ~85 EUR; a la carte ~80–120 EUR per person before wine. A farewell dinner here sends the trip off in style.

Key facts

Location18–19 Parnell Square North, Dublin 1. Basement of the Dublin Writers Museum. 15-minute taxi from St. Stephen’s Green.
HoursDinner: Tuesday–Saturday from 5:30 PM. Closed Sunday & Monday.
ReservationsEssential, especially for Saturday. Book well in advance via chapteronerestaurant.com or call directly.
PriceTasting menu ~85 EUR. A la carte ~80–120 EUR per person before wine. Wine pairings available.
Dress codeSmart casual. No strict dress code but the room invites making an effort.
AccessibilityBasement location accessed by stairs. Contact the restaurant in advance to discuss any mobility requirements — staff are accommodating.

Try something different — alternative plans

If Chapter One isn't the right farewell dinner, here are other strong options for Saturday evening:

🍴For a relaxed evening

The Winding Stair

Modern Irish cooking overlooking the Ha'penny Bridge on Ormond Quay. Daily-changing menu with named Irish producers, farmhouse cheeses, and seasonal fish. More relaxed atmosphere and lower price point (~35–45 EUR mains). A strong farewell dinner without the formality.

🍷For the wine lovers

Dax Restaurant

French-Irish fine dining in a Georgian basement on Pembroke Street Upper. Chef Graham Neville trained in Paris. Refined, intimate room with a strong wine program. Walking distance from St. Stephen's Green. A natural alternative if Chapter One is fully booked.

🌆For a neighborhood feel

Locks Brasserie

Canal-side French-Irish brasserie in Portobello with a terrace overlooking the Grand Canal lock. Classic brasserie cooking — steak frites, fish of the day, proper desserts. A farewell dinner that feels easy rather than formal. ~30–40 EUR mains.

Round 1 — Opening views

Ireland Specialist
Chapter One is a legitimate Dublin landmark. Mickael Viljanen took over from Ross Lewis and immediately earned his own Michelin star. The cooking is world-class, the room is beautiful, and a Saturday night here is a proper way to close a week in Ireland.
Food & Culture Lead
The tasting menu is the way to go for a group. Everyone eats the same courses, the kitchen controls the pacing, and the wine pairing takes the guesswork out of ordering. It becomes a shared experience rather than seven people staring at menus.
Accessibility Advisor
My concern is the stairs. It's a basement restaurant accessed via a staircase. Anyone with mobility challenges should contact the restaurant directly — they're known for being helpful, but there's no lift. Worth a phone call well before the night.
Group Logistics
Saturday night is their busiest service. Book as early as possible — ideally weeks in advance. A 7:00 PM reservation gives the group time to settle in after the drive from Killarney without rushing.

Round 2 — Back-and-forth

Ireland Specialist
Parnell Square is north of the Liffey, about 15 minutes by taxi from St. Stephen's Green. Two taxis or one large one. The area around the restaurant is perfectly safe but not as lively as the south side — plan to taxi back after dinner as well.
Food & Culture Lead
One practical note: the tasting menu runs about 2.5 to 3 hours. If the group wants a nightcap at Kehoe's or The Long Hall afterward, a 7:00 PM start means you'd finish around 9:30–10:00 PM — perfect timing for a last pint.
Accessibility Advisor
After a long drive from Killarney, a 3-hour dinner might feel long for some. The a la carte option is faster — about 90 minutes — if the group prefers a shorter evening. Worth discussing before booking.
Group Logistics
Budget roughly 85 EUR for the tasting menu or 80–120 EUR a la carte per person, plus wine. For seven, expect a total bill of 800–1,200 EUR including drinks and service. Splitting is straightforward.

Revisions agreed by the panel

  • Book a 7:00 PM table for the group on Saturday 9/5 as early as possible. Call or email the restaurant directly.
  • Confirm stair access in advance and discuss any mobility requirements with the restaurant.
  • Decide between the tasting menu (shared experience, ~2.5–3 hours) and a la carte (faster, more flexible) before the night.
  • Arrange taxis to and from Parnell Square — two standard taxis or one large vehicle.
  • Allow time for a nightcap at Kehoe's or The Long Hall after dinner if the group has energy.

Panel's final recommendation

Chapter One is the panel's top pick for the farewell dinner on Saturday 9/5. The Michelin-starred cooking, the atmospheric basement setting, and the sense of occasion make it the right restaurant for the last evening of the trip. Book early, taxi to Parnell Square, and let the kitchen do the work. If Chapter One is fully booked or the group prefers something more relaxed, The Winding Stair and Dax are both excellent alternatives.
