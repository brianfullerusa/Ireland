Sun 8/30 pre-session drinks #11

Bar 1661

Dublin activity review • Sunday 8/30 pre-session cocktails

Snapshot

A poitin-focused cocktail bar on Green Street in Smithfield, Dublin 7. Named after the year the English Crown banned the distillation of poitin — Ireland's original spirit, a potent clear liquor traditionally distilled from grain or potatoes in rural stills. For centuries poitin was made illegally across the Irish countryside; it was only re-legalized in 1997. Bar 1661 is dedicated to reviving and celebrating this spirit through serious craft cocktails in a beautiful, dimly lit space. It sits around the corner from The Cobblestone, making it the perfect pre-trad-session stop.

What it is

Poitin (pronounced "potcheen") is Ireland's oldest spirit — a clear, unaged liquor traditionally distilled from grain, potatoes, or whey in small pot stills across rural Ireland. In 1661, the English Crown declared its production illegal, driving it underground for over three centuries. Generations of Irish families made it in hidden stills in bogs and mountain hollows, and the tradition became part of Irish folklore and identity. When poitin was finally re-legalized in 1997, a new generation of distillers began producing it legally and with craft-level care.

Bar 1661 exists to tell that story through cocktails. The bar is dedicated almost entirely to poitin — they stock multiple legal brands (Bán, Micil, Glendalough, Mad March Hare) and use them as the base for a creative cocktail menu. The drinks are beautifully made, the staff can explain the history and tasting notes of each poitin, and the space itself — dark wood, copper accents, low lighting — creates the feeling of a modern speakeasy with deep Irish roots.

Its location on Green Street in Smithfield puts it around the corner from The Cobblestone, Dublin's best trad music pub. The natural Sunday evening flow is dinner at L. Mulligan Grocer in Stoneybatter, a 10-minute walk to Bar 1661 for cocktails, then around the corner to The Cobblestone for the trad session.

Why it made the list

  • The only poitin-focused bar in Dublin — a unique way to experience Ireland's original and oldest spirit.
  • The history of poitin is a fascinating story that connects to broader themes of Irish identity, rebellion, and revival.
  • Serious craft cocktails — not a novelty bar, but a genuinely well-run cocktail program.
  • Beautiful, atmospheric space that feels like a discovery.
  • Perfect geographic position: 10 minutes from L. Mulligan Grocer, 2 minutes from The Cobblestone.
  • Staff are knowledgeable and enthusiastic about poitin — expect to learn something.

Try something different — alternative plans

If Bar 1661 isn't the right pre-session stop, consider:

🎵Go straight to the music

Skip cocktails, arrive early at The Cobblestone

If the group would rather get settled at The Cobblestone before the session starts, skip Bar 1661 and walk straight from dinner. Arriving at 8:00–8:30 means securing good seats before the 9:00 PM session.

🥃Whiskey instead

Whiskey at L. Mulligan Grocer

Stay at L. Mulligan Grocer after dinner for a round of Irish whiskey from their excellent collection. Same amount of time, fewer venues to navigate, and the whiskey list is one of Dublin's best. Then walk to The Cobblestone.

🗺Explore Smithfield Square

Wander Smithfield before the session

Smithfield Square is a large cobblestoned plaza surrounded by former distillery buildings. Walk through on the way from dinner and absorb the atmosphere. The old Jameson chimney stack is visible from the square. Then into The Cobblestone.
