Killarney & Kerry

Wednesday, September 2 — Friday, September 4, 2026

Killarney sits at the edge of Ireland's first national park — 26,000 acres of ancient oak woodland, three interconnected lakes, and mountains that change color with every shift in weather. The town itself has been welcoming visitors since the 1750s, making it one of the oldest tourist destinations in Ireland, yet it never feels worn out. Red deer roam freely through the parkland (one of the last native herds in Europe), jaunting cars still clip-clop down lanes that haven't been widened since the Victorian era, and the surrounding Kerry countryside — Dingle, the Gap of Dunloe, the Ring of Kerry — delivers some of the most dramatic coastal and mountain scenery anywhere in Europe. Expect extraordinary drives, lakeside walks, and pubs where someone is always playing something.

The pace

Moderate with scenic drives. One major outing per morning, midday hotel rest, then a lighter afternoon activity. Killarney town is compact and walkable for evening pubs and restaurants.

The interests

Dramatic scenery, historic houses, lakeside walks, traditional music, seafood, and the chance to explore Ireland's most famous peninsulas. Golf available at world-class courses nearby.

The base

Three nights in Killarney town, Sep 2–4. Arriving from Galway (~3.5 hours via Limerick). Departing Sep 5 for Dublin.

Thursday 9/3 — National Park Day

Muckross House and the scenic corridor — Torc Waterfall, Ladies' View, Moll's Gap.

02 Morning

Muckross House & Gardens

A magnificent Victorian mansion set on the shores of Muckross Lake in Killarney National Park. The guided house tour, 65 acres of landscaped gardens, and the nearby Traditional Farms make this the essential Killarney heritage stop.

03 Late morning

Torc Waterfall & Ladies' View

A short walk to a dramatic 20-meter waterfall in ancient oak woodland, followed by a drive to Ladies' View — the panoramic overlook Queen Victoria's ladies-in-waiting famously admired in 1861. Moll's Gap completes the scenic trilogy.

Jaunting Car Ride

The iconic Killarney experience. Horse-drawn jaunting cars carry you along the lake, through ancient oak woodlands, and past the ruins of Muckross Abbey. The jarveys are legendary storytellers. Book 2 cars for the group (fits 4 per car).
