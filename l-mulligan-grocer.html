Sun 8/30 dinner #10

L. Mulligan Grocer

Dublin activity review • Sunday 8/30 primary dinner

Snapshot

A gastropub in the heart of Stoneybatter, one of Dublin's oldest and most characterful neighborhoods. L. Mulligan Grocer occupies a converted Victorian grocery shop on Stoneybatter Road — the original shopfront lettering and fittings remain — and serves food that is several levels above standard pub fare. Think bone marrow on toast, duck confit with braised lentils, proper charcuterie boards, and a craft beer and whiskey list that rewards adventurous drinkers. The crowd is overwhelmingly local, the atmosphere is relaxed and unpretentious, and the kitchen takes its work seriously.

What it is

L. Mulligan Grocer opened in 2010 in what was formerly a Victorian-era grocery shop, and the conversion was done with care — the original shopfront, shelving, and hand-painted signage remain intact. It was one of the first pubs in Dublin to take craft beer seriously, and the drinks list still reflects that pioneering spirit: a rotating selection of Irish craft beers on tap, a deep whiskey collection, and natural wines alongside the classics.

But it's the kitchen that sets this place apart from other Dublin pubs. The menu draws on proper technique and quality sourcing — bone marrow, duck confit, house-made charcuterie, and seasonal dishes that change regularly. It's gastropub cooking done right, with none of the pretension that word sometimes implies. You're still in a pub. You're still having pints. The food just happens to be excellent.

Stoneybatter itself is one of Dublin's great neighborhoods — a village within the city, with independent shops, cafes, and a strong community feel. It's north of the Liffey, about a 10-minute walk from Smithfield (where Bar 1661 and The Cobblestone are located), which makes it a natural starting point for Sunday evening before moving to cocktails and trad music.

Modern Irish restaurant overlooking Trinity College. Refined but unpretentious cooking, elevator-accessible upstairs dining room, good wine list. If the group prefers to stay in the city center for dinner, this is the swap. Taxi to Smithfield after for Bar 1661 and The Cobblestone.
