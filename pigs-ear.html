The Pig's Ear

Dublin activity review • Sunday 8/30 alternative dinner (primary: L. Mulligan Grocer)

Snapshot

A modern Irish restaurant on Nassau Street, Dublin 2, with an upstairs dining room that overlooks the front gates of Trinity College. The Pig's Ear has been a Dublin favorite since 2009, known for refined but unpretentious cooking that draws on Irish ingredients and traditions without being bound by them. The menu features dishes like pig's head terrine, roast hake with brown butter, and slow-cooked lamb shoulder. The wine list is well-curated, the service is knowledgeable, and the elevator-accessible upstairs dining room makes it one of the more accessible quality restaurants in the city center.

What it is

The Pig's Ear sits at the junction of Nassau Street and Dawson Street, one of the most central locations in Dublin. The building houses a ground-floor bar area and a first-floor dining room accessed by elevator — a significant advantage in a city where many older restaurants require navigating stairs. The upstairs room is the main event: a warm, contemporary space with large windows that look out over the railings of Trinity College.

The kitchen's approach is modern Irish with a nose-to-tail sensibility — hence the name. Dishes like pig's head terrine, bone marrow, and offal preparations sit alongside more conventional choices like roast hake, lamb shoulder, and excellent steaks. The cooking is refined without being fussy; portions are generous without being excessive. The wine list leans European, with good representation from France and Spain, and the staff are happy to recommend pairings.

As an alternative to L. Mulligan Grocer for Sunday dinner, The Pig's Ear offers a more conventional restaurant experience in a more central location. It trades the neighborhood gastropub atmosphere of Stoneybatter for proximity to the hotel and elevator accessibility — a meaningful consideration depending on the group's needs and energy levels.
