Alt. half/full day #06

Gap of Dunloe

Killarney activity review • Alternative half or full day option

Snapshot

The Gap of Dunloe is a narrow mountain pass carved by glaciers during the last Ice Age, running 11 km between the MacGillycuddy's Reeks (Ireland's highest mountain range) to the west and Purple Mountain to the east. The pass begins at Kate Kearney's Cottage — a pub and tourist stop at the mouth of the gap — and winds through a dramatic valley of five glacial lakes, towering rock walls, and open moorland before reaching the Head of the Gap at the Black Valley. The classic Gap of Dunloe experience combines a jaunting car (or walk/cycle) through the gap with a boat trip back through the three Lakes of Killarney to Ross Castle. This combination — gap and lakes together — is often called the finest outdoor experience in Kerry.

Key facts

LocationKate Kearney's Cottage, 11 km west of Killarney (~15 min drive)
Gap length11 km one way. Walking: 2.5–3 hours. Jaunting car: ~1.5 hours. Cycling: ~1 hour.
Boat tripFrom Head of the Gap (Lord Brandon's Cottage) through the three lakes to Ross Castle. ~1.5 hours. €25–30 per person.
Combined costJaunting car through gap: ~€80–100 per car (fits 4). Boat: €25–30 pp. Total per person: ~€50–55.
DurationFull experience (jaunting car + boat): 4–5 hours. Walking the gap only: 2.5–3 hours one way.
AccessibilityThe road through the gap is unpaved in sections and has a steady gradient. Jaunting cars handle the terrain. Walking requires reasonable fitness. The boat is step-down boarding.

Why we'd want to do this

  • Often described as the single most beautiful experience in Kerry — surpassing even the Ring of Kerry for sheer drama.
  • The combination of jaunting car through a glacial pass followed by a boat through three linked lakes is unique in Ireland.
  • Kate Kearney's Cottage is a proper pub stop with character — great start or finish to the day.
  • The jaunting car option means the scenery comes to you — no hiking required.
  • Less commercialized than the Ring of Kerry. Feels like discovering something special.

What's good to see

  • Kate Kearney's Cottage — the traditional starting point, named after a 19th-century woman who sold poteen (illegal whiskey) to travelers. Good pub food and character.
  • The Wishing Bridge — about 1 km into the gap. Legend says wishes made on the bridge come true.
  • The five glacial lakes along the gap: Coosaun Lough, Black Lake, Cushnavally Lake, Auger Lake, and Black Lough.
  • The Head of the Gap — where the valley opens into the Black Valley, one of the last places in Ireland to get electricity (1976).
  • Lord Brandon's Cottage — a tea room at the Head of the Gap where you board the boat. Simple refreshments in a stunning setting.
  • The boat trip itself: passing under ancient stone bridges, through the Meeting of the Waters where the three lakes connect, with commentary from the boatman on local legends.

Common review themes

What reviewers love

  • "The most magical experience of our entire Ireland trip" is a recurring theme.
  • The jaunting car drivers (jarveys) are praised for storytelling, humor, and local knowledge.
  • The boat trip is universally loved — "floating through the lakes with mountains on every side was breathtaking."
  • September visitors report fewer crowds and often get the gap nearly to themselves.

Common complaints

  • Logistics are complicated — you end up at Ross Castle, not where your car is parked. Requires advance planning or a taxi back.
  • In heavy rain, the gap loses much of its charm and the unpaved sections become muddy.
  • The full experience takes 4–5 hours with no midday hotel rest possible.
  • Jaunting car seats are open-air — dress in layers and bring rain gear regardless of forecast.

Try something different — alternative plans

If the Gap of Dunloe logistics feel too complex, three alternatives that deliver similar magic:

⛳For the golf fans

Beaufort Golf Club — parkland near the Gap

A beautiful parkland course in Beaufort, right at the entrance to the Gap of Dunloe. Play a round while the rest of the group does the gap. Green fees approximately €50–70. Rejoin the group at Ross Castle when the boat arrives. Book at beaufortgolfclub.com.

🍴For the foodies

Kate Kearney's Cottage + Killarney town food walk

Start with a late breakfast or coffee at Kate Kearney's Cottage, walk the first kilometer of the gap to the Wishing Bridge, then return and spend the rest of the day exploring Killarney's food scene: Murphy's Ice Cream, Bricin for boxty, and the Killarney Brewing Company taproom.

🗺For the sightseeing fans

Black Valley drive + Ladies' View loop

Drive into the Black Valley from the Moll's Gap side (paved road, stunning scenery) for a taste of the same landscape the Gap walkers see at the Head of the Gap. Stop at Ladies' View and loop back through Killarney National Park. Less time commitment, similar mountain drama, and you keep your car with you.

Round 1 — Opening views

Ireland Specialist
The Gap of Dunloe is genuinely special. If I could only recommend one outdoor experience in Kerry, this might be it. But the logistics are the challenge. You park at Kate Kearney's, take a jaunting car through the gap, then a boat to Ross Castle — and your car is still at Kate Kearney's. Someone has to sort that out.
Group Logistics
With two cars, it's solvable. Park one at Kate Kearney's and one at Ross Castle. After the boat arrives at Ross Castle, drive back to Kate Kearney's to collect the other car. Or just book a taxi from Ross Castle back to Kate Kearney's — about €20.
Accessibility Advisor
The jaunting car handles the terrain, so nobody has to walk the gap. The boat boarding involves stepping down into the boat, which requires reasonable balance. The boatmen are very experienced at helping passengers. I'm comfortable recommending this for the group.
Food & Culture Lead
Kate Kearney's Cottage is a genuine pub with good food and real atmosphere. It's not a tourist trap. And Lord Brandon's Cottage at the other end does simple tea and scones in a setting you won't believe. The food bookends of this experience are solid.

Round 2 — Back-and-forth

Ireland Specialist
My concern is timing. The full gap-and-boat takes 4–5 hours with no real break in the middle. That's the one day where the midday rest doesn't happen. An alternative: walk just the first section of the gap (Kate Kearney's to the Wishing Bridge, about 30 minutes each way), then do something else in the afternoon.
Group Logistics
I like the half-gap idea as a departure-day option on Saturday morning. Walk to the Wishing Bridge, come back, have tea at Kate Kearney's, then drive to Dublin. It's a taste of the gap without committing a full day.
Food & Culture Lead
If they do the full gap on Friday instead of Dingle, they lose the Slea Head Drive and the Dingle food scene. I'd argue Dingle has more variety for this group — pubs, seafood, history, and scenery all in one package.
Accessibility Advisor
Weather is the deciding factor. The gap is at its most magical in good weather. If Friday dawns clear and still, the gap is transcendent. If it's raining, Dingle is the better call — it has indoor options.

Revisions agreed by the panel

  • Position the Gap of Dunloe as the weather-dependent alternative to Dingle for Friday.
  • If the full gap + boat trip is done: depart by 9:00 AM, use two cars (one at Kate Kearney's, one at Ross Castle).
  • If time is limited: the first section (Kate Kearney's to the Wishing Bridge) takes 30 minutes each way and captures the essence of the gap.
  • Consider a short gap walk on Saturday morning before departing for Dublin.
  • Book the jaunting car and boat in advance via Gap of Dunloe Tours or similar operator.

Panel's final recommendation

The Gap of Dunloe is magnificent but logistically complex for a group that values midday rest. The panel recommends it as the Friday alternative if the weather is excellent and the group is energized, or as a short Saturday-morning walk (Kate Kearney's to the Wishing Bridge) before departing for Dublin. For the default Friday plan, Dingle offers a better balance of scenery, food, culture, and pacing.
