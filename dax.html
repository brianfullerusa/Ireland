Dax Restaurant

Return to Dublin activity review • Saturday 9/5 alternative farewell dinner (primary: Chapter One)

Snapshot

French-Irish fine dining in the basement of a Georgian townhouse on Pembroke Street Upper, just off Fitzwilliam Square. Chef Graham Neville trained in Paris and brings classical French technique to Irish seasonal produce — the result is elegant, confident cooking in an intimate room. Named after the town of Dax in southwest France, the restaurant has been a quiet fixture of Dublin's fine dining scene since 2005. Refined without being fussy, it's a natural fit for anyone who appreciates precise, ingredient-driven cooking.

Key facts

HoursDinner: Tuesday–Saturday from 6:00 PM. Closed Sunday & Monday.

Dress codeSmart casual. The room is elegant — dress accordingly.
AccessibilityBasement level accessed by stairs. Contact the restaurant in advance for any mobility requirements.

Why we'd want to do this

  • Classical French technique applied to Irish produce — a refined, satisfying style of cooking.
  • The Georgian basement setting is intimate and atmospheric, fitting for a farewell dinner.
  • Walking distance from St. Stephen's Green — no taxi needed from most city center hotels.
  • Quieter and less well-known than Chapter One, which often means easier reservations and a more private experience.
  • The wine list leans French and is thoughtfully curated — strong pairing potential.

What's good to see

  • The tasting menu lets the kitchen showcase its range and takes the decision-making off the table.
  • The cheese course draws from both Irish farmhouse and French traditions.
  • The room itself — low ceilings, warm tones, candlelight in a Georgian basement.
  • Seasonal specials that reflect what's coming from Irish farms and fisheries that week.
  • Excellent sommelier guidance for anyone who wants help navigating the wine list.

Common review themes

What reviewers love

  • "Hidden gem" is the most common phrase — many diners feel they've discovered something special.
  • Consistent praise for the quality and precision of the cooking.
  • Service described as "professional but warm" and "unhurried."
  • The intimate size of the room makes every table feel like a good table.

Common complaints

  • The basement location won't suit everyone — stairs and a compact room.
  • Some find it "too quiet" — the intimate setting means the atmosphere depends heavily on the night's crowd.
  • Not as well-known as Chapter One or Patrick Guilbaud, which means less buzz around the name.
  • Portions lean toward elegant rather than generous — fine dining sizing.

Try something different — alternative plans

If Dax isn't the right farewell dinner, here are other strong options for Saturday evening:

⭐For the top-tier experience

Chapter One by Mickael Viljanen

Michelin-starred modern European in the vaulted basement of the Dublin Writers Museum on Parnell Square. The panel's primary recommendation for the farewell dinner. Tasting menu ~85 EUR. Book well in advance for Saturday evening.

🍴For a relaxed evening

The Winding Stair

Modern Irish restaurant overlooking the Ha'penny Bridge. Daily-changing menu, named Irish producers, and a warm room with river views. More relaxed than Dax, with mains at ~35–45 EUR. A strong option if the group prefers casual over formal.

🌆For a neighborhood feel

Locks Brasserie

Canal-side French-Irish brasserie in Portobello with a terrace overlooking the Grand Canal lock. Classic brasserie cooking, good wine list, and a neighborhood atmosphere. ~30–40 EUR mains.

Revisions agreed by the panel

  • Hold Dax as the primary backup if Chapter One is unavailable for Saturday 9/5.
  • Book via dax.ie or call directly — a 7:00 or 7:30 PM reservation is ideal.
  • Confirm stair access and discuss any mobility needs with the restaurant in advance.
  • Consider the tasting menu for a shared group experience; a la carte for flexibility.
  • Walk to Kehoe's or The Long Hall after dinner — both are within 15 minutes on foot.

Panel's final recommendation

Dax is the panel's top alternative to Chapter One for the farewell dinner. The French-Irish cooking is refined, the Georgian basement is atmospheric, and the walking-distance location simplifies logistics. It's the right choice if Chapter One is booked or if the group prefers a quieter, more intimate setting. Book in advance and let the wine list do the talking.
