Wed 9/2 night #07

The Danny Mann

Killarney activity review • Wednesday 9/2 night option

Snapshot

The Danny Mann is a lively, well-loved pub on New Street in Killarney, named after a character from Gerald Griffin's 19th-century novel The Collegians. It has earned a strong reputation for nightly traditional music sessions that bring genuine energy to the room. Where Courtney's is intimate and quiet, the Danny Mann is its more boisterous counterpart — a bigger space with a livelier crowd, louder music, and an atmosphere that gets people tapping their feet and singing along. The pub draws a good mix of locals and visitors, and the musicians are reliable professionals who play every night. It's the kind of place where an evening that was meant to be "just one pint" turns into something longer.

Why it made the list

  • Reliable live traditional music every single night — no guessing whether there will be a session.
  • More energetic atmosphere than Courtney's — better for a group that wants to feel the buzz of a lively pub.
  • Larger space means a group can find seats together more easily.
  • Good mix of locals and visitors keeps the atmosphere authentic without being exclusively touristy.
  • Central location, easy walk from dinner venues and back to accommodation.

Common review themes

What reviewers love

  • "Best trad music in Killarney" appears frequently in reviews — the musicians are consistently praised.
  • The atmosphere is described as "electric" on good nights — the energy of the room lifts everyone.
  • Staff are efficient and friendly even when the pub is packed.
  • A welcoming, unpretentious pub that makes visitors feel like regulars.

Common complaints

  • Gets very loud and crowded during peak music hours — not ideal for quiet conversation.
  • Some reviewers note it can feel more tourist-oriented than Courtney's on busy nights.
  • Seating fills up fast once music starts — arrive before 9:30 for a table.
  • The energy level may be too much after a long day of travel for some.
