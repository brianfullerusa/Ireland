Treyvaud's Restaurant

Killarney activity review • Thursday 9/3 dinner

Snapshot

Treyvaud's is one of Killarney's most popular restaurants, located on High Street and run by brothers Paul and Mark Treyvaud, both classically trained chefs. The restaurant serves modern Irish cooking that showcases local Kerry ingredients — think Kerry lamb, Atlantic seafood, farmhouse cheeses, and seasonal vegetables from local suppliers — prepared with contemporary technique and presented with care. The dining room is bright, warm, and welcoming, with an atmosphere that sits comfortably between casual and fine dining. Treyvaud's has earned a strong reputation locally and nationally, frequently appearing on lists of Ireland's best restaurants outside Dublin. It's the kind of place where the cooking is serious but the atmosphere is not — a relaxed, confident restaurant that knows what it's doing.

Why it made the list

  • One of Killarney's best restaurants — a step up from pub food without crossing into stuffy fine dining.
  • The cooking celebrates Kerry ingredients: local lamb, fresh seafood, farmhouse cheese, seasonal produce.
  • Run by two brothers who are passionate about what they do — the care shows in every dish.
  • The atmosphere balances quality with comfort — a place where you can have an excellent meal in a relaxed setting.
  • Perfect for the Thursday evening after a full day exploring the National Park — a reward for the day's adventures.

Common review themes

What reviewers love

  • "Best meal we had in Ireland" appears repeatedly — the cooking consistently exceeds expectations.
  • The use of local ingredients is praised — reviewers appreciate knowing where the food comes from.
  • Service is described as "friendly but professional" — attentive without being overbearing.
  • The wine list gets strong marks, with staff offering knowledgeable recommendations.

Common complaints

  • Very popular — can be difficult to get a table without booking well in advance.
  • Prices are higher than pub food, though reviewers consistently say it's worth it.
  • The restaurant can feel busy on peak nights — early or late sittings are more relaxed.
  • Portions, while beautifully presented, lean toward restaurant-sized rather than pub-generous.
