Fri 9/4 evening #13

The Cellar at the International Hotel

Killarney activity review • Friday 9/4 final Killarney dinner

Snapshot

The Cellar is a restaurant located in the vaulted basement of the International Hotel, one of Killarney's grand old hotels on Kenmare Place. The stone-walled, atmospheric space — with its low arched ceilings and warm lighting — creates a distinctive dining environment that feels like eating in a medieval wine cellar. The menu features classic bistro-style cooking with strong Kerry ingredients: Kerry beef, local seafood, farmhouse cheeses, and seasonal produce. The wine list benefits from the hotel's cellars and is one of the more extensive in Killarney. As the final dinner in Killarney before departing for Dublin, The Cellar offers a sense of occasion — a place that feels like a proper farewell to Kerry without being overly formal.

  • Strong bistro menu featuring Kerry beef, local seafood, and farmhouse ingredients.
  • The wine list is one of the best in Killarney — a good place to celebrate the Kerry leg of the trip.

Common review themes

What reviewers love

  • The cellar atmosphere is consistently praised — "unlike any other restaurant in Killarney."
  • The Kerry beef dishes get special mention — well-sourced, well-cooked, and satisfying.
  • The wine list is called "impressive" and "well above the usual standard for Killarney."
  • Service is described as attentive and professional without being stuffy.

Common complaints

  • The basement location means stairs — not ideal for everyone. Check accessibility in advance.
  • The space can feel enclosed and warm when busy — the low ceilings are atmospheric but not airy.
  • As a hotel restaurant, some reviewers find it slightly less characterful than standalone restaurants.
  • Prices reflect the hotel setting — higher than pub dining but comparable to Treyvaud's.
